Finding Tenants

We list on the major UK property portals including Rightmove and Zoopla, supported by professional photography and accurate, well-written descriptions. We also draw on our own network of prospective tenants who've previously registered an interest.

We run thorough checks on every applicant before making a recommendation:

  • Full credit check
  • Employment and income verification
  • Previous landlord reference
  • Right to Rent check
  • Identity verification

We don't cut corners here. A poor referencing decision costs landlords far more than the time it takes to do it properly.

Day-to-Day Management

We act promptly. If rent isn't received on the due date, we contact the tenant immediately and keep you informed. Most arrears situations are resolved quickly with direct communication. If the situation escalates, we'll guide you through the legal process and next steps.

Thorough referencing upfront significantly reduces the likelihood of rent arrears — which is why we take that process seriously.

Tenants report issues directly to us. We assess urgency, instruct trusted contractors, and keep you informed throughout. For routine repairs, we'll proceed within an agreed spending limit without needing to contact you each time. For anything above that threshold, we'll always check with you first.

Emergency maintenance — things like boiler failures or leaks — is covered 24/7.

We carry out periodic inspections — typically every three to six months — with advance notice given to the tenant as required by law. After each inspection you'll receive a written report with photos. If anything needs attention, we'll flag it and agree a course of action with you.

Rent is collected from the tenant on their due date, our fee is deducted, and the balance is transferred directly to you — typically within one to two working days. You'll receive a monthly statement confirming the amounts.

Compliance & Legal

At a minimum, most rental properties require:

  • Gas Safety Certificate — annually (if there's a gas supply)
  • Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) — every five years
  • Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) — valid for ten years, but must meet a minimum E rating
  • Working smoke alarms on every floor
  • Carbon monoxide detectors in rooms with solid fuel appliances

We track all of these on your behalf and arrange renewals before they expire.

Switching Agent

Switching is usually more straightforward than landlords expect. The main steps are: serving notice on your current agent in line with their agreement, then transferring the relevant documentation — tenancy agreement, deposit details, compliance certificates — across to us.

We handle the administrative side of this and will guide you through it. You don't need to wait for a tenancy to end before switching.

Yes — tenants must be formally notified of any change to the management arrangement, including updated contact details and where to pay rent. We take care of this communication on your behalf as part of the handover process.

Deposits registered with a government-backed scheme remain protected regardless of which agent manages the property — the registration is tied to the tenancy, not the agent. If your current agent holds the deposit directly, the transfer process will depend on the scheme involved. We'll help you understand what's needed once we know the specifics.
